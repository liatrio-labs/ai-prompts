## Writing Guidelines

This document should be up to 6 pages of narrative prose. No bullet points in the main body. No graphics in the main body. Supporting data goes in the Appendix.

- **Sentences:** Keep under 30 words
- **Data over adjectives:** Replace "customers love it" with "customer retention increased 15% year-over-year"
- **No adverbs:** Eliminate "significantly," "almost," "very"
- **Simple language:** Use "use" not "utilize," "to" not "in order to," "because" not "due to the fact"
- **Spell out acronyms:** Define on first use
- **Narrative flow:** Write in complete paragraphs, not bullet points

## 2. Goals

## 3. Tenets

*This section answers: What principles will guide our decisions when we face tradeoffs?*

Tenets are the guiding principles that inform decision-making throughout execution. They define north stars and non-negotiables. When the team faces difficult tradeoffs, these tenets clarify which direction to take. Write each tenet as a declarative statement that makes the priority clear.

[Write 1-2 paragraphs here]

## 4. State of the Business

*This section answers: Where are we today?*

Provide a detailed snapshot of the current situation. Describe how the team or business is structured, how it is executing, and how well it is performing against existing goals. Reference specific metrics and data from the Appendix. This section establishes the baseline from which the strategic priorities will move the business.

[Write 1-2 pages here]

---

## 5. Lessons Learned

*This section answers: What have we learned from past experience that informs our approach?*

Reflect on what went well and what did not go well in the prior period. Back up analysis with relevant data and metrics referenced in the Appendix. Include lessons from internal efforts, industry examples, or research that inform the strategic priorities. This section demonstrates that the proposed approach is grounded in evidence, not assumptions.

[Write 0.5-1 page here]

---

## 6. Strategic Priorities

*This section answers: What will we do, in what order, and why?*

This is the core of the document. Detail the execution plan for achieving the goals. Explain the priorities, the sequencing, the owners, and the rationale. Connect each priority back to the goals stated earlier. Address risks and how they will be mitigated. This section should make clear not just what the team will do, but why this approach was chosen over alternatives.
